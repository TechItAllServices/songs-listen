I endure, not out of hope,

but because my fate is chained to another.

We are two bound by consequence,

tethered like stones in a sinking river-

if one sinks, the other is pulled under.


They do not wish for my end.

Their voice, quiet but certain, calls me back from the edge.

Not love nor lust

But a bond forged in shared despair. And fear-

fear of what would follow if I were gone.


Yet even as they beg me to remain,

their path winds toward death.

Each step they take, knowingly or not,

cuts the thread that holds us both.

They do not see it.

Or perhaps they do,

but cannot stop the motion.


Still, I remain.

Not for joy. Not for peace.

But because they are the last post holding up the structure.

And if they fall,

the whole crumbles with them.


So I stand in the shadow of their undoing,

held back by duty,

bound by shared consequence,

waiting to see which of us breaks first.
